## GB's seed (challenge / sharpen / refute)
**`/goal`'s only fit is bounded EXPLORATION + ANALYSIS with a verifiable stopping condition — never generation, design, or judgment.** Match it to its strengths (tireless broad cross-referencing, inventorying, gap-finding, task-ordering across the whole federation overnight) and fence it from its weaknesses (prose quality, design, deciding — where it bloats and slops). Concretely:

- **Nightly per-title PACKET.** Each night, `/goal` emits ONE small, schema-bounded packet per title — *specified analysis, not prose*: e.g. `{title, book↔code coherence drift, open B32 obligations, Atrium state, distribution status, top-N ordered next-tasks with effort, LGP-alignment note, confidence}`. Hard token cap per packet; verifiable stop = (N titles × bounded schema), nothing open-ended.
- **Subordinate by construction.** The packet is **input to the cockpit**, never an action. Read-at-night / **propose-only / zero writes** (the night-watch posture). Humans + GB gate; `/goal` never decides, authors, or commits.
- **Cross-functional.** It explores what a single-lane view misses — Book↔Code, Atrium, B32 ledger, distribution, LGP — and orders the resulting work; it does not do the work.
- **Lifecycle fit:** `/goal` owns only the cheap **EXPLORE** stage, feeding the human-gated **design → build → publish → wide-distribute** stages. It surfaces and orders; the rails (with the V1 craft bar) execute.
- **The fit-gate (KM's law):** assign `/goal` only tasks where a goal-structured methodology yields *optimal* quality — objective output + verifiable stop (inventory, cross-check, gap-list, task-order). If the task is subjective craft (authoring, the visual bar, design), `/goal` is the wrong tool — don't attempt it there.
