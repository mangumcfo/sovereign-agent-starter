## R1 — The Review-Ready Contract (machine-checkable; no book reaches KM without it)
A book is "ready to review" iff ALL of:
1. **Boards executed** — Editorial, UX, Technical (the multi-persona board machinery): each board emits an *extensive enhancement list*; every finding becomes an **obligation** (B32/hopper packet) with owner + status.
2. **Obligations closed** — every board finding `closed` or `deferred` with explicit reason. No silent drops; deferred items appear in the Brief.
3. **Fidelity gate passed** — GB witness trace (the standing seeit-pattern gate): every claim/citation/derived artifact in the book traces to its sealed source; semantic match, not just hash match (the seeit FAIL lesson: cryptographic integrity is necessary, NOT sufficient).
4. **Review Brief sealed** (one page, on top): what changed since last human touch · what the boards caught and closed (counts + highlights) · deferred items with reasons · **the 3–7 genuine judgment calls** that need KM's eyes — each framed as a decision, not a discovery.
**Target:** KM review = few hours of judgment, zero detection. If a pilot review surfaces detection work the gates missed, that gap becomes a contract amendment (the rail hardens by being used).

## R2 — Atrium Loop-Closure Audit (the 100%-cockpit fix)
**The one primitive, everywhere:** anything KM sees carries a disposition (Accept / Reject / Comment) → disposition becomes a packet → packet has owner + status → KM watches it close — **without leaving Atrium, without relaying through Tiger.**
- **Audit (GB lane):** inventory every existing Atrium surface against one question — *can KM disposition here, and does it visibly close?* Output = gap list, each gap an obligation. Audit-first, fix-by-obligation, **zero rebuilds** — nothing already built breaks.
- **Build (Tiger lane):** close the gaps; clone existing patterns (Review group / KDP Dispatch accept-loop) rather than invent.
- **Includes:** real-time rendering review of books in-Atrium; issue intake in-Atrium (problems become packets, not conversations).
- **Acceptance:** KM completes one full book-review cycle (R3) start-to-finish inside Atrium, every disposition closing visibly.
