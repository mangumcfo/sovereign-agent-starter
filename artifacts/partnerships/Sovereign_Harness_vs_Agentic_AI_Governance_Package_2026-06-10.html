1 · Executive Summary

Same conviction, two depths

Decidr MCP and the Sovereign Harness answer the same emerging need: governed decision infrastructure that keeps a human in command of AI agents — turning conversational generation into reviewable, accountable decisions with an audit trail. The alignment in philosophy is real and unusual: both treat human judgment as the scarce resource to protect, both make proposals reviewable before commitment, and both keep a record of what was decided.

They differ in depth and scope. Decidr is a lightweight, MCP-native commercial tool that bridges Claude conversations into governed initiatives and reviewable decisions — a fast, low-friction on-ramp. The Sovereign Harness is a full-stack sovereign substrate: receipted obligations on a hash-chained ledger, structural human gates (breath-gates), a cockpit of review surfaces (Atrium), a sovereign execution node, constitutional invariants enforced in the runtime, cryptographic anchoring, federation across nodes, and a living book↔code source of truth — designed for fiduciary, regulated, and multi-generational contexts.

The opportunity: these are complementary, not competing. A Decidr-class package is exactly the kind of human-friendly surface that could license and layer on top of our sovereign primitives — gaining verifiable governance, cryptographic auditability, and rigor it would otherwise have to build — while we keep the foundational research, the constitutional core, and the sovereign node as retained IP. This document maps where the two align, where ours goes deeper, and how a collaboration could be structured.

Already running — not a deck

The harness is proving value in live testbeds today: (1) a regulated quality-systems consultancy (welding coordination / QMS) — AI-assisted plan generation and gap analysis against real standards, an interactive natural-language edit loop with status tracking, and human engineering sign-off as the final gate, producing exportable signed artifacts with full provenance; and (2) a collective economic coordination primitive — privacy-preserving pooling of procurement intent, agent swarms surfacing optimized paths with full handoff traces, and an explicit human gate on any material commitment. Both demonstrate the stack handling real "should we proceed?" decisions with receipts. (Source: prepared Agentic AI Governance Package — Financial Case.)

3 · Detailed Alignment & Differences

Where the concepts match

  • Reviewable proposals before commitment. Decidr's "reviewable decisions" map directly onto our obligation packets + breath-gate — a proposal is examined and dispositioned before it becomes action.
  • Human primacy. Both insist a human ratifies; agents inform, humans decide. Our K1 makes this a structural runtime guarantee rather than a workflow convention.
  • From unstructured input to governed unit. Decidr "a few sentences → a governed initiative" is the same move as our capture → packet → validate → gate governed loop.
  • Audit trail. Both keep a record of decisions; ours is hash-chained, receipted, and independently replayable.
  • MCP common ground. Decidr is MCP-native; our node exposes a clean API contract and can speak MCP — a natural technical bridge.

  • Living book↔code source. Our governance is derived from published books that are the spec; a Book↔Code tree audits that every module traces to a chapter, and surfaces render from sealed text — provable, not asserted.
  • Helix rendering & derived surfaces. Operator docs (seeit) and review surfaces are rendered from sealed sources, auto-refreshing — no parallel canon to drift.
  • Merkle anchors & receipted memory. A tamper-evident, hash-chained record with cryptographic anchoring; you can prove what happened years later.
  • K1–K4 constitutional invariants. Human-Primacy, Default-Deny, Audit-Immutable, Constitution-Validated-Extension — enforced server-side as structural properties, defensible to a board or regulator in ~60 seconds on the operator's own node.
  • Federation resonance. Sovereign nodes coordinate by signed resonance, not central control — multi-party governance without merging chains.
  • Generational continuity. The whole stack is scored against Lasting Generational Prosperity and built to be inherited across stewards.
  • Rigor standards (no rubber stamps). A Review-Ready Rail with machine-checked board rigor (LGP alignment per finding, findings→obligations, depth gate, fidelity witness, human-sense test) — the antidote to "correct-but-hollow" output.
4 · Collaboration Paths

How a Decidr-class package could layer on our primitives

The cleanest model: the commercial package keeps its fast, friendly surface and licenses the sovereign substrate underneath — gaining verifiable governance and auditability it would otherwise build from scratch, while our core IP (constitutional design, sovereign node source, book↔code research) stays retained.

Technical integration points

  • B32 packets + obligation ledger: their initiatives mint B32 obligation packets on our hash-chained ledger — typed, receipted, evidence-tiered decision records.
  • MCP seam: expose our Node API + ledger as an MCP server; Decidr-style agents emit proposals straight into our governed loop.
  • Atrium cockpit: white-label or co-branded review surfaces (Awaiting-Me gate, board review, coherence monitor) over their initiatives.
  • Helix rendering: their decision docs render from sealed sources (render-not-recreate) — operator-facing surfaces that can't drift from the source.
  • Merkle enforcement: decisions anchor to our Merkle/hash-chain so each record is tamper-evident and independently replayable.

Value flow (backer / royalty)

  • Licensing/royalty on the sovereign substrate embedded in the commercial package.
  • Equity or revenue share in a separate commercial vehicle (the Agentic AI Governance Package entity) — preferred for aligned capital.
  • Performance participation tied to governed "proceed" outcomes (fewer false starts, faster ratified projects, auditable sign-off).
  • Future on-chain yield instruments linked to the governance packages, once regulatory clarity exists.

5 · Risks & Recommendations

Where shallow work creeps in — and how our rigor strengthens the offering

Risk — "correct but hollow." A lightweight decision bridge can ship outputs that look governed (a record exists, a human clicked approve) but are shallow — no depth check, no source trace, no proof the decision was substantive. At scale this compounds into GIGO and erodes trust exactly where accountability was the selling point.

Recommendation. Our Board Rigor Standard and validation/fidelity harness are the structural antidote — and a differentiator a partner could license: every material finding becomes a tracked obligation, every claim must trace to a resolving source, a depth gate forbids silent "all clear," and an independent witness verifies before anything reaches the human. This is what turns "an audit trail" into "verifiable governance."

IP & sovereignty protection. Retain the sovereign node source, constitutional design, and book↔code research in the primary entity; license only the production surfaces, templates, and coordination modules. The Controlled-Link Doctrine (published artifacts point only at surfaces we control) keeps the partner's deployments dependent on our living, governed source — not a fork.
